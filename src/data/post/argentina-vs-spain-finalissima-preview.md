The ultimate clash of footballing styles is finally here. Copa América holders Argentina meet Euro champions Spain in a highly anticipated Finalissima. This match pits South American grit and experience against European technical fluidity and youth. 

Both nations dominated their respective continental tournaments to earn this spot. This preview breaks down the form, squads, tactical battles, and predictions for the match.

---

## Form and Recent History

Argentina enters this fixture with supreme confidence after retaining their Copa América title. Lionel Scaloni's side has lost just once in their last twenty matches, showing incredible defensive solidity and tactical flexibility. They continue to combine possession control with a lethal threat on the counter-attack, much like the systems analyzed in our [tactical analysis guide](/tactics-in-depth-football-strategy/).

Spain has been equally impressive under Luis de la Fuente. The European champions went undefeated in their Euro campaign, defeating hosts Germany, France, and England along the way. Their style is direct, utilizing explosive wingers to stretch opponents and create central spaces.

## Team News

Argentina will field their strongest available starting eleven. Lionel Messi remains the focal point of the attack, though Alexis Mac Allister and Rodrigo De Paul will carry the physical workload in midfield. Scaloni has no major injury concerns, allowing him to choose between Lautaro Martínez and Julián Álvarez to lead the line.

Spain will be without midfielder Gavi, who continues his recovery from a long-term knee injury. However, Rodri remains the undisputed anchor of the midfield, flanked by the creative Fabián Ruiz. Up front, Spain will rely on their sensational teenage winger Lamine Yamal and the experienced Nico Williams on the opposite flank.

## Predicted Lineups

Both coaches will deploy their familiar systems. Scaloni is expected to setup Argentina in a balanced 4-3-3 that easily transitions into a compact 4-4-2 out of possession. De la Fuente will counter with Spain's trademark high-pressing 4-3-3.

## Tactical Battle

The match will be decided in the wide areas. Spain's wingers, Lamine Yamal and Nico Williams, will test Argentina's fullbacks with their raw pace and dribbling ability. Argentina must double up in wide zones to prevent Spain from creating isolated one-on-one situations.

In midfield, the battle for possession will be fierce. Rodri will look to control the tempo for Spain, while De Paul and Mac Allister will aim to disrupt his rhythm with aggressive pressing. If Argentina can win the ball in midfield, Messi will have the space to release Lautaro Martínez behind Spain's high defensive line.

---

## Players to Watch

For Argentina, **Alexis Mac Allister** is the key link between defense and attack. His ability to resist Spain's high press and distribute clean passes will dictate Argentina's offensive flow. Additionally, his defensive work rate will help protect the backline.

For Spain, teenage sensation **Lamine Yamal** is the main creative spark. His vision, dribbling, and crossing from the right wing will keep Argentina's defense under constant pressure. If Yamal finds his rhythm early, Spain will dominate the attacking third.

---

## Match Prediction

We expect a highly tactical and closely contested match. Spain will likely control the majority of possession, testing Argentina's compact defensive shape. However, Argentina's tournament experience and clinical counter-attacking ability should give them the edge in decisive moments.

Argentina will exploit Spain's high line with direct passes to Lautaro Martínez. Spain will create chances through Yamal, but Emiliano Martínez should prove difficult to beat in goal.

**Prediction:** Argentina 2 - 1 Spain

## FAQ

### When and where is the Finalissima being played?
The official venue and date are yet to be finalized by UEFA and CONMEBOL, but the match is expected to take place in a major European stadium.

### Who won the last Finalissima?
Argentina won the last Finalissima in 2022, defeating Euro champions Italy 3-0 at Wembley Stadium.

### What happens if the match ends in a draw?
If the score is tied after 90 minutes, the match will go straight to a penalty shootout without playing extra time.
