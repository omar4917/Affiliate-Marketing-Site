## First Half: Tactical Standoff and Openers

Spain started the match with their signature high-tempo passing, pushing Argentina deep into their own half. Nico Williams caused early problems on the left flank, forcing a smart save from Emiliano Martínez in the 12th minute.

Argentina responded in the 28th minute when Lionel Messi released Julián Álvarez with a defense-splitting pass. Álvarez composed himself and slotted the ball past David Raya to give Argentina the lead.

Spain equalized just before halftime through Lamine Yamal. The young winger cut inside from the right and curled a brilliant strike into the top corner.

## Second Half: Tension and Late Drama

The second half began with both teams adopting a more cautious approach to avoid errors. The midfield battle intensified as Rodrigo De Paul and Rodri clashed repeatedly for possession.

Spain took the lead in the 72nd minute when Dani Olmo tapped in a low cross from Marc Cucurella. The European champions looked poised to secure the trophy as the match entered its final minutes.

However, Argentina equalized in the 89th minute. Lautaro Martínez volleyed home a cross from Alexis Mac Allister after a chaotic set-piece scramble.

## Penalty Shootout and Decisive Moments

With no extra time scheduled, the match went straight to a penalty shootout. Emiliano Martínez once again proved to be Argentina's shootout hero.

Martínez saved two penalties, denying Spain's Dani Olmo and Aymeric Laporte. Gonzalo Montiel scored the winning penalty to secure the trophy for Argentina, mirroring his World Cup heroics.
